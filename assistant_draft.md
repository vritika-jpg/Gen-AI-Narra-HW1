# About Me

I am an MS student at Johns Hopkins University in Intelligent Systems and Applied AI (ISAI), and I am passionate about using technology to solve meaningful, real-world problems. I enjoy combining analytical thinking with practical implementation, especially in projects that blend machine learning, data-driven reasoning, and scalable systems.

A problem area I care deeply about is improving data science and automation workflows across organizations. Teams often lose significant time to repetitive data preparation, manual reporting, and fragmented processes, which slows innovation and limits decision quality. I am especially interested in building AI-enabled systems that reduce this friction and make high-impact analysis faster and more reliable.

- Use generative AI to automate repetitive data cleaning, transformation, and documentation tasks.
- Use generative AI to draft clear summaries of analyses, trends, and key findings for technical and non-technical stakeholders.
- Use generative AI to speed up experimentation by generating workflow templates, baseline models, and comparison reports.
